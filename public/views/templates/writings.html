STEMentor User Guide
Version 1.4 (Updated 3-8-2017)

Welcome to STEMentor! This application is fairly straightforward, and you may be able to achieve full functionality without the need for this guide. If you are having any issues, or simply want to make sure you are getting the most from your STEMentor experience, please read on!

This guide is spearated into two sections, based on if you are using STEMentor as a mentor or as a student.


For Mentors

Mentor Profile Creation:

1) You will need a google (gmail) account. The picture associated with this account will be used for your STEMentor profile, so make sure it is the picture you want.

2) Go to http://stementor.herokuapp.com and click “Become a Mentor.” You will be prompted to sign in with your google account. Click “Log In As Mentor” to do so.

3) If this is the first time you have logged in with this google account, you will be prompted to fill out your profile. Clicking “Go to Your Profile” will take you right there.

4) Once on your profile page, click “Edit Your Profile” and you can now fill in information in a number of input fields. When you are done, click “Save Changes.” Your name will now show up in the results when someone searches for a mentor and students will be able to send you messages.

5) You can go to your profile at any time by clicking “Your Profile” in the navigation bar at the top of the window. You can edit your profile information as much as needed. There is also a tab for FAQs—click on that to bring up the FAQs section, which can be edited in the same way as your profile.

Mentor Messaging:

1) When you receive a new message from a student, an email notification will be sent to the gmail account associated with your STEMentor profile. In this email will be a link that will take you right to your STEMentor inbox. You can also visit your inbox at any time while in the STEMentor application by clicking “Inbox” in the navigation bar at the top of the window.

2) Unread messages will be displayed first in your inbox. Below these will be messages that have already been read, indicated by grey coloring. Clicking on any message will display it in a pop-up window, where it can be read and replied to.

3) Type your reply and click “Send Message.” The student will be notified by email that their question has been responded to.


For Students

Searching for a Mentor:

1) You will be able to search for mentors and view their profiles without logging in to STEMentor. On the homepage, click “Find a Mentor” to go to the search page. Here, you can search for mentors by keyword in the main search bar, or click “Refine Your Search” to search by specific criteria.

2) Searching for a mentor will return a list of cards that display some information from a mentor’s profile. You can click on the icon that looks like two people in the lower-left corner of each card to view that mentor’s full profile. Clicking on the message icon in the lower right corner of a mentor’s card or clicking on the “Ask Me A Question” button on a mentor’s profile will both begin the messaging process, but you will need to be logged in to send a message.

Student Account Creation:

Creating a STEMentor account is fast and easy. All you will need is a google (gmail) account. Click “Log In / Sign Up” in the upper right of the navigation bar, then click “Log In As Student.” Trying to click a button to message a mentor when you are not logged in will also direct you to a log in pop-up.

Student Messaging:

1) Once logged in, you can message a mentor by clicking on the message icon in the lower right corner of a mentor’s card or clicking on the “Ask Me A Question” button on a mentor’s profile.

2) Fill in the fields and click “Send Message.” The mentor will be notified by email that they have received a new message.

3) When the mentor responds, an email notification will be sent to the gmail account associated with your STEMentor account. In this email will be a link that will take you right to your STEMentor inbox. You can also visit your inbox at any time while in the STEMentor application by clicking “Inbox” in the navigation bar at the top of the window.

4) Messages are sorted into two categories: those that the mentor has responded to (listed first), and those that have not yet been responded to (listed second). Click on a message to display it in a pop-up window.


Additional Notes

Once a google account has been used to log in, the Firebase authentication system remembers this email address. This means that the log in pop-up will appear and close quickly on subsequent log ins. If you are logged in to your browser with your google account (in Chrome, your name will appear in the upper right corner of the browser window), and you have logged in to STEMentor previously, you will be logged in automatically when you go to the STEMentor application.

Having two tabs open in one window, both accessing STEMentor, can cause issues. The same may be true using two windows on one computer.
